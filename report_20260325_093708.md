## Executive Summary
Our Meta Ads campaign spent $6,598.62 from March 1 to March 21, resulting in 1,707 conversions and a return on ad spend (ROAS) of 3.04. The overall click-through rate (CTR) was 2.26%, with an average cost per click (CPC) of $0.24. We've identified areas of strength and weakness in our campaigns, which will inform our next steps.

## What's Working
The "Prospecting - Lookalike 1%" campaign has the highest ROAS at 3.24, indicating effective targeting. The "Catalog Sales - All Products" campaign has the highest CTR at 2.91% and the lowest CPC at $0.11. The "Brand Awareness - Video" campaign has the highest spend at $1,539.80, but still maintains a respectable CTR of 2.29% and CPC of $0.24.

## What Needs Attention
The "Retargeting - Website Visitors" campaign has the lowest CTR at 0.87% and the highest CPC at $0.72, indicating potential issues with ad relevance or targeting. The "Lead Gen - Free Trial" campaign has a relatively low CTR at 2.05% and a high CPC at $0.44, suggesting room for improvement in ad creative or bidding strategy.

## Recommended Actions
1. **Optimize the "Retargeting - Website Visitors" campaign**: Review ad creative, targeting, and bidding strategy to improve CTR and reduce CPC. Consider pausing the campaign if issues persist.
2. **Refine the "Lead Gen - Free Trial" campaign**: Test new ad creative, adjust bidding strategy, and consider targeting adjustments to improve CTR and reduce CPC.
3. **Scale the "Prospecting - Lookalike 1%" campaign**: Allocate additional budget to this high-performing campaign to maximize ROAS and conversion volume.
4. **Monitor and adjust the "Brand Awareness - Video" campaign**: Continue to monitor spend, CTR, and CPC, and adjust targeting or ad creative as needed to maintain efficiency.
5. **Analyze and apply learnings from top-performing campaigns**: Identify common characteristics and strategies among top-performing campaigns and apply these insights to underperforming campaigns.
